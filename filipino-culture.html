👥 Who Are the Filipino People?

Filipinos are unique. Some are products of interracial marriages called mestizos. Their physical features reflect their mixed ancestry.

The majority of Filipinos consist of: Tagalog, Visayan, Ilocano, Bicolano, Pampangueño, Panggasinense, Ibanag (Cagayano), and Zambal.

There are also many other ethnic/indigenous groups found across the country.

🏔️ Indigenous Groups of Luzon

Ifugao & Kalinga

📍 Mountain Province

Led by chieftains. Practice marriage feasts & burial rites. Use the gangsa instrument. Wear colorful traditional clothing.

Isnegs

📍 Apayao River

Use kaingin method of farming. Led by Kamenglan (the brave). Society allows polygamy.

Tinguians

📍 Abra

Plant rice in lowlands and mountain terraces. Women wear beads and tattoos on their arms.

Hanunuo Mangyan

📍 Oriental Mindoro

Write in the Buhid script. Preserved culture by isolating from lowland influences.

🌊 Indigenous Groups of Visayas

Negritos (Atis)

📍 Visayas

Known for Ati-Atihan festival – a thanksgiving for bountiful harvest. Live by hunting and fishing. Called Mamanua (Leyte/Samar), Magahat (Negros), Corolanos/Bukidnon (Panay).

🌿 Indigenous Groups of Mindanao

Badjaos & Tausugs

📍 Mindanao & Sulu

Muslim Filipinos. Good swimmers & divers. Earn by diving for pearls. Called "sea gypsies."

Samals

📍 Sulu–Zamboanga

Live by fishing, farming, and trading.

Yakans

📍 Basilan

Very good horse riders who travel mostly on horseback.

Maranaos

📍 Lanao

Known for beautiful brass works and the Sarimanok. Wear colorful sarong, malong, and kupia.

🙏 Religion & 🗣️ Language

Religion: The Philippines is the only predominantly Catholic nation in Asia. About 90% are Roman Catholics. The rest are non-Catholics: Muslims, Iglesia ni Cristo, Aglipayan, Evangelists, and other Christian faiths.

Language: Official languages: Filipino and English. Most widely spoken native language: Cebuano, followed by Tagalog. Other major languages: Ilocano, Hiligaynon, Bicol, Waray, Pampango, Pangasinense.

🏛️ Heritage Sites & Events

Heritage sites must meet the criteria set by World Heritage Convention members to be included in the World Heritage list.

🏘️ Historic City of Vigan (Calle Crisologo)

The Mestizo Section, house of Father Burgos and Leona Florentino — declared the National Cultural Treasure of the Philippines.

⛪ Baroque Churches

San Agustin (Manila), Sta. Maria (Ilocos Sur), Paoay (Ilocos Norte), Miagao (Iloilo) — all considered Cultural Treasures of the Philippines.

📌 Key Terms to Remember
Bayanihan

Filipino spirit of communal unity and cooperation (damayan) — helping others without expecting anything in return.

Gangsa

A musical instrument used by the Ifugao and Kalinga during special celebrations.

Kaingin

A method of farming practiced by the Isnegs.

Buhid

The ancient Filipino alphabet still used by the Hanunuo Mangyans.

Ati-Atihan

A colorful Negrito festival celebrated as a thanksgiving for a bountiful harvest.

Sarimanok

A famous symbol/art form of the Maranao people of Lanao.

Ningas-Kugon

Starting a task with enthusiasm but losing energy and not finishing well.

Pagmamano

A Filipino gesture of respect, pressing the back of an elder's hand to one's forehead.

💡 Remember: The different indigenous groups have their own customs and traditions, but all Filipinos share a common heritage!
