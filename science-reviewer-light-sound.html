Properties of Light
Reflection, refraction, dispersion & more!
✨ What is Light?
📖
Definition: A form of energy that allows us to see objects!
📏
Travel: Moves in a straight line and can travel through empty space (vacuum)
🚀
Speed: 300,000 km per second! That's super fast!
🪞 1. Reflection
The bouncing of light off a surface.

🔑 Angle of reflection = angle of incidence
Mirrors Shiny metal Water
🌊 2. Refraction
The bending of light when it moves from one medium to another. Light changes speed!
Air → Water Coin looks closer in water
🌈 3. Dispersion
White light separates into colors like a rainbow!

ROYGBIV:
R O Y G B I V
🔍 Materials & Light
🪟
Transparent Lets ALL light pass — clear glass, clean water
🧊
Translucent Lets SOME light pass — wax paper, frosted glass
🧱
Opaque BLOCKS all light — wood, metal, walls
🎨 Colors of Light
🔴🟢🔵
Primary: Red + Green + Blue
⚪
Combined: All three together = White Light!
😎 Protecting Your Eyes
  • 1 Do not look directly at the sun
  • 2 Wear sunglasses in bright sunlight
  • 3 Keep distance from TV and phone screens
  • 4 Take breaks when using computers

Properties of Sound
Vibration, pitch, frequency, and loudness!
🎵 What is Sound?
📖
Definition: Energy produced by vibrating objects!
〰️
Vibration: Back-and-forth movement of an object
🌍
Travels through: SolidLiquidGas (air)
⚡ Speed of Sound
🥇
Fastest: Solid — like metal rails!
🥈
Medium: Liquid — like water
🥉
Slowest: Gas — like air
🎶 Pitch
How high or low a sound is. Depends on frequency!

📈 High frequency → High pitch
📉 Low frequency → Low pitch
〰️ Frequency
Number of sound waves per second.
Measured in Hertz (Hz)!

Infrasonic: 0–20 Hz
Sonic: 20–20,000 Hz
Ultrasonic: >20,000 Hz
📢 Loudness
How strong or soft a sound is.
Depends on amplitude.
Measured in decibels (dB)!
👂 Types of Sound by Frequency
🐋
Infrasonic 0–20 Hz — Too low for humans. Example: whale sounds
🗣️
Sonic 20–20,000 Hz — Normal human hearing. Example: talking!
🐕
Ultrasonic Above 20,000 Hz — Too high for humans. Example: dog whistles!
📊 Loudness Scale (dB)
0–30 dB
🤫 Very quiet
31–50 dB
🌿 Quiet
51–70 dB
💬 Normal
71–90 dB
📣 Loud
91–110 dB
😬 Very loud
111+ dB
💥 Painful!
🛡️ Protecting Your Hearing
  • 1 Cover your ears during very loud noise
  • 2 Avoid listening to headphones at full volume
  • 3 Use ear muffs or ear protection
  • 4 Stay away from very noisy places
