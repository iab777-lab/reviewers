Priest · Common Priesthood · Marriage · Family

Lesson 14 · Things to Remember
Mission of the Priest
▼
  • Grace is the help that God gives so that we can become his children (see CCC 1996).
  • To be sanctified means to "live with God and act with his love" (see CCC 2000).
  • A priest is an essential coworker of the bishop in teaching, sanctifying, and governing Christian communities.
  • The three primary functions of a priest are "to preach the Gospel, shepherd the faithful, and celebrate the Holy Eucharist."
  • Priests are the living and clear image of Jesus Christ.
  • Jesus also called men to be with him.
  • Priests are at the service of mediating the encounter between humanity and God.
  • Priests share in the priesthood of Jesus Christ through the Sacrament of Holy Orders.
  • Priests are the ministers of sanctification of God's people.
  • The priest represents Jesus Christ. He continues the mission of Jesus through the "word" and the "sacrament."

Lesson 15 · Things to Remember
Jesus' Priesthood / Common Priesthood
▼
  • All Christians are also called to serve in God's name and for the salvation of others.
  • We became a member of the People of God during baptism.
  • As the People of God, our head is Jesus Christ.
  • Jesus Christ is anointed by the Father with the Holy Spirit and is established as priest, prophet, and king.
  • As the People of God, we share in Jesus' priesthood.
  • Our acts of charity make us "another Christ" for other people.
  • To practice our "common priesthood" means to represent Jesus Christ to the world faithfully.
  • As the People of God who share Jesus' priesthood, we are all called to offer spiritual sacrifices.
  • We can seek and receive God's help through the sacraments.
  • In the Holy Mass, the Church gathers to remember and to represent the sacrifice of Christ, which we share through the action of the priest and the power of the Holy Spirit.

Lesson 16 · Things to Remember
Marriage in God's Plan
▼
  • God created the first humans — Adam and Eve — in his image of love (see Gen 2:22, CCC 1604).
  • God created a woman as a suitable partner for a man.
  • Marriage between a man and a woman has an important place in God's plan.
  • Sin destroys the human family and prevents us from becoming the "image and likeness of God."
  • Jesus was born to a family (see Lk 2:1-20) where he grew in wisdom.
  • Jesus died on the cross so that we might die to sin and live an upright life.
  • It is Jesus who redeemed the family and reconciled humankind to God.
  • Marriage between a man and a woman is forever, just as God had planned from the very beginning.
  • The union of a man with a woman and their commitment to each other reflects God's faithful love (see CCC 1603).
  • Marriage between a man and a woman is a sign of God's love and faithfulness (see CCC 1617).
  • Through God's grace, "man and woman can achieve the union of their lives for which God created them" (see CCC 1608).

Lesson 17 · Things to Remember
Sacrament of Matrimony
▼
  • God created man and woman to be together (see CCC 371).
  • The vocation of married couples is to create human life and be stewards of creation (see CCC 372).
  • Sin prevented the "first parents" from fulfilling God's original plan.
  • God the Father sent Jesus, his only Son, to redeem the world.
  • Jesus was born of a woman and grew in wisdom with a human family.
  • Jesus taught that marriage is forever.
  • Marriage is a sign of the saving presence of Christ.
  • Divine love is the total giving of self. It is an offering that brings new life to the world.
  • A generous kind of love can be seen in the Christian family.
  • Married Christian couples need God's grace to practice divine love.
  • Jesus instituted the Sacrament of Matrimony.
  • Jesus helps couples to love each other with deep and holy love.
  • Christian marriage's grace is a fruit of Christ's cross (CCC 1615).
  • Christian marriage is also a sign of God's love for the world.
  • Just as the married couple is faithful to each other, Jesus is also devoted to the Church.
  • Christian marriage is Jesus' sacrament of love for his people.

Lesson 19 · Things to Remember
Children's Mission & Family Values
▼
  • Filipinos are family-oriented (see CFC 34).
  • In the family, a person finds belongingness, security, and self-identity.
  • We grow up, nurtured, and supported by the trust, love, and fidelity of our family.
  • As young Christian disciples, we learned about Jesus first through our family.
  • Children help build the Christian family "through love, respect, and obedience towards their parents" (St. Pope John Paul II, 1981, para. 21).
  • In the family, we experience freedom and obligation (see CFC 146).
  • We can genuinely make the family a "communion of persons" by showing them love and care and mutual service every day.
  • The Church teaches us to reject "exclusivism," that is, being concerned only with our own family (see CFC 296).
  • We have to recognize and respond to our responsibilities in the community.
  • By serving our fellowmen, we imitate Christ Jesus our Lord (see CFC 699).

Lesson 20 · Things to Remember
Role of the Family in Society
▼
❓ Why is the family called the vital cell of society?
The family is the "first and vital cell of the society" (CCC 2207) because the family is the beginning of life. It is where children grow through the care and love of their parents. Each family member is being nourished and developed to be a mature individual.
❓ What are the Christian duties of a family?
Families have the duty to proclaim the Gospels not only within their family but also to society. Each family has the responsibility to be morally upright and show love and compassion to their neighbors especially, the least, the lost, and the last.
❓ What are some of the examples of Filipino values?
Filipinos are innately family-oriented, helpful, respectful, and optimistic. Other values include being hospitable, friendly, religious, and sociable.
