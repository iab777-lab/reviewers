What is Topography?

Topography is the arrangement of the natural and artificial physical features of an area. The Philippine topography is varied and rich, with landforms and water forms in different forms and sizes, abundant in natural resources.

üèîÔ∏è Different Landforms

1. Plain

  • Description: A landform that is wide, low, and flat
  • Example: Central plain in Luzon
  • Uses: Rice fields, planting rice, fruit-bearing trees, vegetables, and medicinal plants

2. Mountain

  • Description: A very high landform
  • Examples: Mt. Makiling, Mt. Halcon, Mt. Apo (tallest)
  • Mountain Ranges: Sierra Madre Mountains, Caraballo Mountains
  • Resources: Tropical rainforests, wood, wildlife, minerals (gold, silver, copper, iron)

3. Hill

  • Description: A high landform but lower and smaller than a mountain
  • Uses: Growing plants, raising animals (goats, cattle, sheep)

4. Valley

  • Description: A level landform between ranges of hills or mountains
  • Examples: Trinidad Valley, Cagayan Valley
  • Crops: Palay, corn, root crops, vegetables
  • Animals: Chickens, cows, carabaos

5. Plateau

  • Description: A flat high ground
  • Example: Baguio City in Benguet
  • Features: Cool climate, tourist destination, fresh vegetables and fruits

6. Volcano

  • Description: A mountain with an opening (crater) on top
  • When erupts: Smoke, hot lava, and stones thrown out
  • Examples: Mayon Volcano, Taal Volcano, Mount Pinatubo

7. Peninsula

  • Description: A protruding land along shores, almost surrounded by water
  • Examples: Bicol, Bataan, Zamboanga

üåä Different Water Forms

1. Ocean

  • Description: The largest body of water, very deep and wide
  • Water type: Salty
  • Example: Pacific Ocean
  • Uses: Transportation for ships carrying travelers and goods

2. Sea

  • Description: A big body of water, not as deep and wide as ocean
  • Water type: Salty
  • Uses: Fishing (fish, shrimp, squid), pearl diving
  • Philippine Seas: Philippine Sea (east), West Philippine Sea (west), Sulu Sea (southwest), Celebes Sea

3. Lake

  • Description: A body of water surrounded by land
  • Water type: Not salty, does not flow to the sea
  • Example: Taal Lake
  • Uses: Source of fish income

4. River

  • Description: A natural-flowing water form, usually freshwater
  • Source: Tributaries
  • Example: Loboc River
  • Resources: Fish, shrimp, crabs, mussels

5. Waterfall

  • Description: Water falls from a high place
  • Uses: Tourist spots
  • Examples: Pagsanjan Falls, Maria Cristina Falls

6. Spring

  • Description: Water comes out from the ground
  • Types: Hot and cold springs
  • Example: Los Ba√±os, Laguna (hot springs)
  • Uses: Taking baths, hot spring resorts

7. Bay

  • Description: Part of the sea but reaches into the land
  • Example: Manila Bay
  • Uses: Ships anchor, strolling, watching sunset

8. Strait

  • Description: Water passage between islands
  • Examples: San Bernardino Strait, Verde Island Passage, Surigao Strait, Mindoro Strait

üîë Key Facts to Remember

  • Tallest Mountain: Mt. Apo
  • Philippine Deep: Approximately 10,430 m (34,219 ft) deep
  • Mountain Ranges in Luzon: Sierra Madre, Caraballo
  • Minerals from Mountains: Gold, silver, copper, iron
  • Peninsula Examples: Bicol, Bataan, Zamboanga
  • Valleys: Trinidad Valley, Cagayan Valley
