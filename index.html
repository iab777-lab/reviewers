Mathematics

Master numbers, operations, and problem solving

```
1
Place Value & Number Sense
▶

What is Place Value?

Place value tells us the value of each digit based on its position in a number.

Ones
1
Tens
10
Hundreds
100
Thousands
1,000
Example: In the number 3,482 — the digit 3 is in the Thousands place (value = 3,000), 4 is in the Hundreds place (value = 400), 8 is in the Tens place (value = 80), and 2 is in the Ones place (value = 2).
💡 Study TipUse a place value chart and practice writing numbers in expanded form: 3,482 = 3,000 + 400 + 80 + 2.
2
Fractions
▶

Parts of a Fraction

Numerator
Top Number
Denominator
Bottom Number
  • Proper fraction: numerator < denominator (e.g., ¾)
  • Improper fraction: numerator > denominator (e.g., 7/4)
  • Mixed number: whole + fraction (e.g., 1¾)
  • Like fractions: same denominator (e.g., ⅓ and 2/3)
  • Unlike fractions: different denominators (e.g., ½ and ⅓)
⚡ Quick Check
Which is an improper fraction?
3
Basic Operations
▶

The Four Operations

  • Addition (+): Combines two or more numbers. Result = Sum
  • Subtraction (−): Finds the difference between numbers. Result = Difference
  • Multiplication (×): Repeated addition. Result = Product
  • Division (÷): Splits into equal groups. Result = Quotient
Order of Operations (PEMDAS): Parentheses → Exponents → Multiplication & Division → Addition & Subtraction. Always work left to right within the same level.
4
Geometry Basics
▶

Shapes & Formulas

Square Area
s × s
Rectangle Area
l × w
Triangle Area
½ × b × h
Circle Area
π × r²
  • Perimeter: Total distance around a shape (add all sides)
  • Area: Amount of space inside a shape
  • Volume: Amount of space inside a 3D figure (l × w × h for rectangular prism)
5
Angles
▶

What is an Angle?

An angle is formed when two rays meet at a common point called the vertex. Angles are measured in degrees (°).

Acute Angle
Less than 90°
Right Angle
Exactly 90°
Obtuse Angle
90° to 180°
Straight Angle
Exactly 180°
Reflex Angle
180° to 360°
Full Angle
Exactly 360°

Special Angle Pairs

  • Complementary angles: Two angles that add up to 90° (e.g., 30° + 60°)
  • Supplementary angles: Two angles that add up to 180° (e.g., 110° + 70°)
  • Adjacent angles: Two angles that share a common side and vertex
  • Vertical angles: Opposite angles formed by two intersecting lines — they are always equal
Tool: A protractor is used to measure angles. Place the center hole on the vertex and align one ray with the 0° line, then read the degree where the other ray points.
⚡ Quick Check
An angle that measures 135° is called a _____ angle.
💡 Study TipUse the letter "L" to remember a right angle — it always looks like a perfect corner (90°). Anything smaller is acute, anything bigger (up to 180°) is obtuse!

Science

Explore living things, matter, energy & the Earth

```
1
Living Things & Their Needs
▶

Characteristics of Living Things

  • Made of one or more cells
  • Need food, water, and air to survive
  • Can grow and develop
  • Can respond to their environment
  • Can reproduce (make offspring)
  • Produce and use energy
Classification: Scientists group living things into Kingdoms: Animals, Plants, Fungi, Protists, and Bacteria (Monera).
💡 Study TipRemember the 7 characteristics with "MRS GREN": Movement, Respiration, Sensitivity, Growth, Reproduction, Excretion, Nutrition.
2
Matter & Its Properties
▶

States of Matter

Solid
Fixed shape & volume
Liquid
Fixed volume, no fixed shape
Gas
No fixed shape or volume
  • Physical change: Changes shape/size but NOT the substance (e.g., cutting paper, melting ice)
  • Chemical change: Forms a NEW substance (e.g., burning wood, rusting metal)
⚡ Quick Check
Which is an example of a chemical change?
3
Energy & Its Forms
▶

Forms of Energy

  • Light energy: from the sun, can travel through space
  • Heat (thermal) energy: produced by moving particles
  • Sound energy: produced by vibrations, needs a medium to travel
  • Electrical energy: movement of electrons
  • Mechanical energy: energy of motion (kinetic) + stored energy (potential)
  • Chemical energy: stored in food and fuel
Energy can be transferred from one object to another and converted from one form to another. Example: Chemical energy in food → Mechanical energy when we move.
4
Earth & Space
▶

The Solar System

  • The Sun is the center of our solar system — it provides light and heat
  • 8 planets orbit the Sun: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune
  • Earth rotates on its axis causing day and night
  • Earth revolves around the Sun causing the four seasons
  • The Moon orbits Earth and causes tides and the phases of the moon
💡 Study TipRemember the planets in order: "My Very Excellent Mother Just Served Us Nachos" (Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune)
```

English

Grammar, reading, vocabulary & writing skills

```
1
Parts of Speech
▶

The 8 Parts of Speech

  • Noun: Names a person, place, thing, or idea (e.g., teacher, school, book)
  • Pronoun: Replaces a noun (e.g., he, she, it, they)
  • Verb: Shows action or state of being (e.g., run, jump, is, are)
  • Adjective: Describes a noun (e.g., big, beautiful, cold)
  • Adverb: Describes a verb, adjective, or another adverb (e.g., quickly, very)
  • Preposition: Shows relationship (e.g., in, on, under, beside)
  • Conjunction: Connects words or clauses (e.g., and, but, or, because)
  • Interjection: Expresses emotion (e.g., Oh! Wow! Hurray!)
⚡ Quick Check
In "The big dog runs fast," what part of speech is "fast"?
2
Sentence Structure
▶

Parts of a Sentence

  • Subject: Who or what the sentence is about
  • Predicate: What the subject does or is
  • Object: Receives the action of the verb
Types of Sentences:
• Declarative – makes a statement (ends with period)
• Interrogative – asks a question (ends with ?)
• Imperative – gives a command (ends with period or !)
• Exclamatory – shows strong emotion (ends with !)
3
Vocabulary & Context Clues
▶

Types of Context Clues

  • Definition clue: The meaning is directly stated in the sentence
  • Synonym clue: A word with similar meaning is nearby
  • Antonym clue: A word with opposite meaning gives contrast
  • Example clue: Examples around the word hint at its meaning
  • Inference clue: You use logic from the rest of the sentence
💡 Study TipWhen you don't know a word, cover it, read the whole sentence, then make a guess based on the surrounding words before checking a dictionary.
4
Reading Comprehension
▶

Key Reading Skills

  • Main idea: The most important point the author is making
  • Supporting details: Facts and examples that support the main idea
  • Cause and effect: Why something happened (cause) and what happened (effect)
  • Compare and contrast: Finding similarities and differences
  • Inference: Reading between the lines — using clues to figure out unstated meaning
  • Sequencing: Putting events in the order they happened
```

GRMC

Good Manners, Right Conduct & Values Education

```
1
Core Values & Character
▶

Filipino Core Values

  • Pagmamahal sa Diyos (Love of God): Putting God above everything; living a faithful life
  • Pagmamahal sa Pamilya (Love of Family): Caring and respecting one's family
  • Pagmamahal sa Kapwa (Love of Neighbor): Treating others with kindness and empathy
  • Pagmamahal sa Bansa (Love of Country): Patriotism and pride in the Philippines
  • Katapatan (Honesty/Loyalty): Being truthful and dependable in all things
  • Paggalang (Respect): Showing consideration for others, especially elders
💡 Study TipGood values are not just memorized — they are practiced daily. Think about one value you showed today!
2
Good Manners in Daily Life
▶

Practicing Good Manners

  • At home: Help with chores, obey parents, speak politely
  • In school: Listen to teachers, respect classmates, avoid cheating
  • In public: Queue properly, don't litter, give way to the elderly
  • Online: Be respectful in messages, don't share others' private info, avoid cyberbullying
  • Magic words: "Please," "Thank you," "I'm sorry," "Excuse me," "You're welcome"
⚡ Quick Check
You accidentally bump into someone. What should you say?
3
Rights & Responsibilities
▶

Children's Rights (UNCRC)

  • Right to life, survival, and development
  • Right to a name and nationality
  • Right to education
  • Right to health and medical care
  • Right to play and leisure
  • Right to be protected from abuse and exploitation
With rights come responsibilities: study hard, respect others, help at home, obey rules, protect the environment.
4
Citizenship & Community
▶

Being a Good Citizen

  • Obey laws and rules of the school, home, and community
  • Respect authority figures — teachers, parents, government officials
  • Participate in community activities and help others
  • Care for the environment — don't litter, conserve water, plant trees
  • Be responsible with your words and actions, especially online
  • Practice honesty in everything you do
```

Filipino

Gramatika, pagbabasa, pagsulat at bokabularyo

```
1
Bahagi ng Pananalita
▶

Mga Uri ng Salita

  • Pangngalan: Pangalan ng tao, lugar, bagay, o ideya (hal.: guro, paaralan, aklat)
  • Panghalip: Pumapalit sa pangngalan (hal.: ako, ikaw, siya, sila)
  • Pandiwa: Nagpapahayag ng kilos o kalagayan (hal.: kumain, tumatakbo, mahal)
  • Pang-uri: Naglalarawan sa pangngalan (hal.: malaki, maganda, mabait)
  • Pang-abay: Naglalarawan sa pandiwa (hal.: mabilis, dahan-dahan, palagi)
  • Pangatnig: Nag-uugnay ng mga salita o pangungusap (hal.: at, pero, kaya)
  • Pantukoy: Ginagamit bago ang pangngalan (hal.: ang, mga, si, sina)
⚡ Mabilis na Pagsubok
Sa pangungusap na "Mabilis na tumakbo ang bata," ano ang pang-abay?
2
Uri ng Pangungusap
▶

Apat na Uri ng Pangungusap

  • Pasalaysay: Nagbibigay ng impormasyon. Nagtatapos sa tuldok (.)
    Hal.: Masaya ang mga bata sa paaralan.
  • Patanong: Nagtatanong. Nagtatapos sa tandang pananong (?)
    Hal.: Nasaan ang aking lapis?
  • Pautos: Nagbibigay ng utos o pakiusap. Nagtatapos sa tuldok o padamdam (.!)
    Hal.: Buksan ang inyong mga aklat.
  • Padamdam: Nagpapahayag ng damdamin. Nagtatapos sa tandang padamdam (!)
    Hal.: Ang ganda ng panahon ngayon!
3
Bokabularyo at Salitang-Ugat
▶

Pagbubuo ng Salita

  • Salitang-ugat: Pinakasimpleng anyo ng salita (hal.: aral, sulat, laro)
  • Unlapi: Idinaragdag sa simula (hal.: mag- → magaral; um- → umali)
  • Gitlapi: Idinaragdag sa gitna (hal.: -in- → sumulat; -um- → kumain)
  • Hulapi: Idinaragdag sa dulo (hal.: -an → sulatan; -in → basahin)
Kambal-katinig: Dalawang katinig na magkasamang tumutunog sa simula ng pantig (hal.: tr, gr, pr, bl, cl).
💡 PaalalaAng pagsasanay ng pagbabasa araw-araw ay malaking tulong upang mapaunlad ang bokabularyo at pag-unawa.
4
Pagbabasa at Pag-unawa
▶

Estratehiya sa Pagbabasa

  • Pangunahing Ideya: Ang pinakamahalagang mensahe ng teksto
  • Suportang Detalye: Mga katibayan na nagpapatunay sa pangunahing ideya
  • Pagkakasunod-sunod ng Pangyayari: Una, pangalawa, pangatlo, panghuli
  • Sanhi at Bunga: Ang dahilan (sanhi) at ang resulta (bunga) ng isang pangyayari
  • Paghahambing: Paghahanap ng pagkakatulad at pagkakaiba
